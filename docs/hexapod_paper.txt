The Offset Model of a Hexapod
Robot and the Effect of
the Offset Parameter
Kazi Mostafa, National Sun Yat-sen University, Taiwan
Innchyn Her, National Sun Yat-sen University, Taiwan
Yi-Hsien Wu, National Sun Yat-sen University, Taiwan

ABSTRACT
Generally, it is claimed that hexapod walking robots are superior to others. However, in some conditions
hexapod suffers from stability problems. To solve the problem of stability, this paper proposes a new gait model
of hexapod robot named offset model and also investigates the effects of morphological factor of hexapod
robots on their locomotion. A comparison between the offset model and general model of hexapod robot is
also included. The stability margin and error margin are used to indicate the stability of a hexapod robot,
as it walks with different gaits in arbitrary directions. Two hexapod gaits are compared, which are the diametrical gait and the paired metachronal gait. The former is an artificial gait and the latter is a natural gait.
The authors conclude that that the stability of a hexapod robot with the diametrical gait can be enhanced by
increasing the offset parameter.

Diametrical Gait, Error Margin, Hexapods, Offset Model, Paired Metachronal Gait, Stability

The hexapod robot has a symmetrical
structure.
The contact between the leg and the ground
is a point.
There is no sliding phenomenon between
the leg and the ground.
The weight of each leg merges into the
body, and the center of gravity is on the
position of the geometric center.
During the movement, the speed of the
hexapod robot body and the average speed
of each leg are all constant.

The offset model is proposed under the
same assumptions in this paper. We added an
offset parameter D to the middle legs of the
general model to let the relative position between
each standpoint and the center of gravity be
more distributed like the models in Figure 1.

Stability and Error Margin
The stability of walking robots is one of the
significant factors for the design of robotic gaits.
Song and Waldron (1987) defined the stability
margin (sm) as the shortest distance of the center
of gravity to the support polygon. The bigger
the stability margin means the better the stability. In 1998, Yang and Kim (1998) applied the
stability margin to the fault tolerant gait of the
hexapod robot. Chu and Pang (2002) calculated
the stability margin of the hexagonal model and
compared with the rectangular model. The result
shows that the hexagonal robots have better
stability margin than the rectangular robots.
Liu (2009) defined the fault tolerant point
as the intersection of the diagonal lines of a
support polygon during the movement of the
hexapod robot with a pair of leg lifting. The
distance between the center of gravity and the
fault tolerant point is defined as the error margin
(em). A smaller error margin means a better the
stability. When the error margin becomes zero,
the center of gravity is on the intersection of

with diametrical gait and set the parameters of
the general model P = Q = U1=1. Thus, the
parameter D of the offset model will be D =
1 – U2. Figure 3 shows the stability margin of
the general model with the parameters P = Q =
U1= 1 and the offset model with the parameters
P = Q = 1, U2= 2/3, and D = 1/3. We choose the
minimum value of sm to compare the stability
of two models. As seen from the figure, the
parameter sm of the offset model is bigger than
the general model in the first and third stage,
but smaller in the second stage. Figure 4 shows
the stability margin of the offset model with the
parameters P = Q = 1, D + U = 1, and D changes
from 0 to 1 during each moving stage. As seen

from the figure, the stability margin increased
with the increase of the parameter D in the first
and third stage, but decrease in the second stage
under the condition of U + D = 1.
The stability margin will not be changed
when the parameter D increased to 0.064. With
D changes from 0 to 1, the minimum value of
sm increases from 0.2 to 0.364. The result shows
that the stability of the offset model is higher
than general model, and it will become higher
with the increase of the parameter D. As for

DISCUSSION

ACKNOWLEDGMENT

We can enhance the stability of hexapod robot
by increasing the stability margin or decreasing
the error margin. From figure we can see that,
under the same condition of stable area, the stability margin increased in first and third portion
with the increase of the parameter D. Besides,
the error margin decreased with the increase
of parameter D. Therefore, it is recommended
to use the offset model than the general model
while walking toward the X direction with the
diametrical gait.

CONCLUSION
This paper demonstrated the use of an offset
robot model in the assessment of the effects of
altering the offset parameter D. We can utilize
the offset model and change the offset parameter
to zero if we want to use the general model. And
the comparison between two models in this
paper showed that the robots with offset model
have better stability margin than general model
especially for the diametrical gait. Two paired
hexapod gaits walking in two axial directions
were compared. When a diametrical gait used
for X-directional (forward) walking, enlarging the offset parameter D can stabilize the
robot. When this gait is used for Y-directional
(sideway) walking, increasing D enhances the
stability. However, making D larger is more
effective for forward walking. When the paired
metachronal gait is used, increasing D does not
help for either X- or Y-directional walking.
The paired metachronal gait was quite inert to
the change of parameter D. The gait analysis
and simulation of hexapod offset model have
been carried out with different gaits in arbitrary
directions, which have shown that the stability
of hexapod enhanced by increasing the offset
parameter. It is anticipated that the findings can
help designers in determining the configurations, and gaits, of robots.
